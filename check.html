The Science Behind Physiotherapy: Evidence-Based Practice

Dr. B. Chowdhury (PT)'s 25+ years of clinical experience is grounded in solid scientific principles and evidence-based practice. Understanding the scientific foundation of physiotherapy helps patients appreciate why his treatments are so consistently effective.

Fundamental Principles of Physiotherapy

1. Neuroplasticity and Motor Learning

The brain's ability to reorganize and form new neural connections allows for recovery after injury. Dr. Biswanath Chowdhury utilizes this principle in designing progressive exercise programs that retrain movement patterns.

2. Tissue Healing and Adaptation

Understanding the three phases of tissue healing (inflammatory, proliferative, and remodeling) enables Dr. B. Chowdhury (PT) to time interventions appropriately for optimal recovery.

3. Pain Gate Theory

Manual therapy techniques and electrotherapy modalities work by activating large nerve fibers that can "close the gate" on pain signals, providing effective pain relief.

4. Biomechanical Principles

Correcting faulty movement patterns and improving biomechanics reduces stress on injured tissues and prevents re-injury, principles Dr. Biswanath Chowdhury applies in every treatment plan.
